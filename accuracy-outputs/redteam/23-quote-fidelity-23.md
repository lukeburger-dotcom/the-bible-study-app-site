## What I can confidently tell you

John Chrysostom (c. 349–407) preached a series of homilies on the Gospel of John, and the opening verses receive extended treatment early in that series — his discussion of "In the beginning was the Word" falls in the vicinity of **Homily 2** on the Gospel of John (this numbering is standard in the Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers series, First Series, volume 14). In that homily and the ones immediately around it, Chrysostom is preoccupied with using John 1:1 against Arian readings of the Son as a created being — he presses hard on "was" (ἦν) in "In the beginning **was** the Word" as indicating eternal pre-existence rather than a beginning in time, and on "the Word was God" as securing full deity against any subordinationist reading.
