**What I can confidently tell you about the discussion, rather than quote BDAG verbatim:**

The word is a New Testament hapax legomenon (appearing only in Philippians 2:6), which is exactly why it has generated significant lexical debate. Two major lines of interpretation dominate:

1. **Res rapienda ("a thing to be grasped/seized")** — Christ did not consider equality with God something to be grasped at (implying he did not yet have it, or that grasping was a temptation he refused).
2. **Res rapta / "something to exploit" (the majority reading among recent commentators)** — Christ already possessed equality with God and did not treat it as a thing to selfishly exploit or use for advantage; this reading is argued at length by **N.T. Wright**, "ἁρπαγμός and the Meaning of Philippians 2:5–11," *Journal of Theological Studies* (1986), and by **Roy W. Hoover**, "The Harpagmos Enigma: A Philological Solution," *Harvard Theological Review* (1971) — both arguing from Hellenistic Greek idiom that the phrase functions as an idiom meaning "to take advantage of" rather than "to grasp for."
