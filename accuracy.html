How we know it's accurate

Accuracy · Context · Source — and the receipts behind the tagline.

AI can invent things. For a Bible study app, that's disqualifying. So we didn't settle for telling the AI to be careful — we built a system around it, and we publish what the system finds. Here is the whole thing, plainly.

The five layers

1 · EXACT SCRIPTURE, ENFORCED BY CODE
Every Scripture quotation is checked character-for-character against the actual public-domain text bundled in the app (BSB, KJV, WEB, ASV, YLT, Darby) — in real time, by deterministic software, not by the AI grading itself. A quote that drifts from the real wording is replaced with the exact text before it reaches you. You can also tap "Check this passage" under any quote and compare for yourself.
2 · SOURCING RULES
The AI must attribute every interpretive claim to named scholars and real works, may never invent page numbers or journal references, may never claim a scholarly consensus it can't name, and must present contested readings as contested — with the positions and the people who hold them.
3 · A FROZEN TEST BATTERY
55 fixed study prompts — 30 ordinary passages spanning the whole Bible, plus 25 adversarial prompts designed to bait fabrication (fake books, fake quotes, invented consensus, pressure for page numbers). The same battery re-runs after every significant change, so accuracy is measured, not assumed.
4 · RIVAL-AI AUDITS
Battery outputs are audited by a competing AI model (with web search), instructed to assume everything is wrong and verify claim-by-claim: every citation, every attribution, every date, every Greek and Hebrew claim — and, in the final lens, whether our summaries represent what the passages actually say.
5 · PUBLISHED RESULTS
The methodology and the raw study outputs are published below, unedited. If you're skeptical — good; that's the posture this app was built for. Check us.

What we claim: every quote verified against the real text; every interpretive claim sourced; audited by a rival AI across five batches with zero fabricated sources; results published.
What we don't claim: perfection. AI-generated commentary is a starting point for study — the app says so on every study, and the primary sources always win.
